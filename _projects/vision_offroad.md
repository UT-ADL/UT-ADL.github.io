The project aims to demonstrate feasibility of off-road autonomous navigation using vision-based sensors with imprecise satellite navigation support. Challenges addressed include: real-time navigation decisions without pre-mapped areas; outdated maps that don't reflect current environmental conditions; determining passability through puddles or tall grass using camera data rather than LIDAR.

The methodology uses a two-phase approach: dataset collection for training machine learning models (waypoint proposal and destination-optimization), followed by real-robot implementation.

**Funding:** 49,500 EUR (AIRE demonstration project, AI & Robotics Estonia — EDIH, co-financed by the European Union's "European Digital Innovation Hubs" program, project 101083677, and Estonia's Ministry of Economic Affairs and Communications)

**Duration:** April 1, 2023 – December 31, 2023

**Partner:** Milrem Robotics
