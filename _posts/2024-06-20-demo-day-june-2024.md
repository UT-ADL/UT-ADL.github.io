On June 18th, the Autonomous Driving Lab held a Demo Day, showcasing the latest advancements and projects from its members.

## Presentations

**Allan Mitt** demonstrated his work mapping Tartu city in the CARLA simulator using drone footage. He highlighted how "the simulation is remarkable — so much so that you might even spot your own parked car" in the virtual recreation. This technology could enable driving schools to practice complex intersections and potentially serve as a platform for driver exams.

**Mahir Gulzar** presented his testing of route scenarios in CARLA, explaining how "different types of scenarios on a single route" can now be simulated with automatic trigger points initiating various scenarios, rather than testing each separately.

**Tambet Matiisen** shared experiments with "bag scenarios," proposing the inclusion of dynamic obstacles from ROS bag file recordings in the Autoware Mini environment. This approach requires minimal computational resources, making it accessible for regular laptops, though performance metrics need further development.

**Dmytro Zabolotnii** discussed his pedestrian prediction work, covering machine learning implementation, novel performance metrics, and results from database analysis and real-life experiments.

**Edgar Sepp** demonstrated real-time parameter experimentation with bag scenarios, showcasing how different settings affect simulation outcomes and generating significant audience interest.

**Syeda Zillay Nain Zukhraf** presented localization methods using point clouds for GPS-denied environments, outlining her methodology, progress, and validation plans using LIDAR technology.

**Arun Singh** concluded the event by sharing insights from the International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA), sparking discussion that continued into a BBQ celebration.
