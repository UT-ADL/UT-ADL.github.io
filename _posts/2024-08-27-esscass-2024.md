The Autonomous Driving Lab participated in the 21st Estonian Summer School on Computer and Systems Science (ESSCaSS), which took place from August 11-15 at Roosta Holiday Village. Throughout the five-day event, attendees explored machine learning and robotics subjects while engaging in outdoor activities and relaxation.

The lab made meaningful contributions to the program. During the poster session, they presented a simulation demonstration and provided vehicle demonstrations to interested participants. Tambet Matiisen delivered a presentation examining "the End-to-End vs. Modular Dilemma in Autonomous Driving." The team also showcased visual navigation using a Jackal robot.

The event featured a special guest lecturer, Dhruv Shah, who was invited to present a two-part lecture on large-scale machine learning applications for robotics. The summer school proved successful overall, with the lab expressing enthusiasm for participating in future editions of the event.
